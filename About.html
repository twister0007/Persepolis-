Feminist Theory in Persepolis: Exploring Patriarchal Power Structures

Group D is examining how Marjane Satrapi's Persepolis reveals the operation of patriarchal power structures within society. Through the lens of feminist theory, we will analyze how these structures manifest through institutions, individuals, and the acceptance of social norms. Our exploration will focus on the extent to which the text supports or challenges these structures.

Chapters Under Analysis

  • The Pill
    • Exploration of reproductive rights and control over women's bodies.
    • How societal and institutional norms dictate women's autonomy.
  • The Vegetable
    • The impact of patriarchal structures on personal relationships and identity.
    • Resistance to or compliance with gendered expectations.
  • The Horse
    • Symbolism and representation of power dynamics within the family and society.
    • The intersection of gender, class, and political power.
  • Hide and Seek
    • The role of surveillance and control in enforcing patriarchal norms.
    • Individual agency versus societal constraints.

Our Approach

Using feminist theory as a framework, we will dissect these chapters to uncover how Persepolis critiques or reinforces patriarchal systems. By examining the text's portrayal of institutions, individual actions, and societal norms, we aim to highlight the complexities of gender dynamics in the context of the Iranian Revolution and beyond.
